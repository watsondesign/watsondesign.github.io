HIGHSNOBIETY
REDESIGN

Highsnobiety resonates with me as a magazine because of the ecletic, and progressive culture that the magazine embodies. I wanted to capture and maintain the written aesthetic by creating a compelling design to compliment the content.

I began by refreshing the logo. The current logo is a symbolic representation of a wax seal with a striking crown placed in the center. The crown exhibited merit, and so it was kept. The wax profile was removed and the middle inverted. The mark was reduced to a clean, easy to read symbol that preserves the iconic character of the original.

Iterations upon iterations of the word mark in conjunction with the logo were the next steps taken. I initially explored blackletter style fonts. Highsnobiety is a European publication out of Germany and it seemed fitting to pay homage to the countries creation of that style. I even considered creating a custom contemporary Fraktur-styled masthead. After consulting my professor and exploring a variety of fonts including serif and sans-serifed fonts I chose a Requium, a font that "celebrates Renaissance humanism", to portray the brand as traditional, and yet high class.

MALCOLM GLADWELL
BOOK COVERS

After exploring and iterating upon many ideas I figured out I wanted to create something both interesting and conceptual. I looked towards swiss design from the mid-century and began to explore how I could communicate abstract concepts embedded within the books through form, shape, and color.

The red dot you see in the same location on every book is symbolic of the individuals within society who make key differences in each of the novels. The lines are representative of the different psychological worlds around them that Malcolm Gladwell explores in his writing.
